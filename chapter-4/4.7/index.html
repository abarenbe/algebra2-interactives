Section 4.7: Transformations of Polynomial Functions

Learn how to transform polynomial functions by shifting, reflecting, and stretching. Understanding transformations allows you to graph complex functions by starting with a parent function and applying systematic changes.

Recommended Learning Path

  1. Start with Vertical Shifts - the simplest transformation
  2. Learn Horizontal Shifts - watch for the sign change!
  3. Master Reflections - over x-axis and y-axis
  4. Understand Stretches & Compressions - vertical and horizontal
  5. Combine everything in Combined Transformations

Vertical Shifts

Move graphs up or down by adding or subtracting constants.

LearnSee PracticeDo
  • f(x) + k shifts up k units
  • f(x) - k shifts down k units
Start Learning

Horizontal Shifts

Move graphs left or right - watch the sign!

LearnSee PracticeDo
  • f(x - h) shifts right h units
  • f(x + h) shifts left h units
Start Learning

Reflections

Flip graphs over the x-axis or y-axis.

LearnSee PracticeDo
  • -f(x) reflects over x-axis
  • f(-x) reflects over y-axis
Start Learning

Stretches & Compressions

Make graphs taller/shorter or wider/narrower.

LearnSee PracticeDo
  • a·f(x) vertical stretch/compress
  • f(bx) horizontal stretch/compress
Start Learning
