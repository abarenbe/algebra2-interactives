Understanding Reflections

Two Types of Reflections

There are two main reflections for polynomial functions:

Reflection over x-axis

y = -f(x)

Multiply the entire function by -1
Flips the graph vertically

Reflection over y-axis

y = f(-x)

Replace x with -x
Flips the graph horizontally

Key Points

  • -f(x): All y-values become their opposites (3 → -3)
  • f(-x): The graph is mirrored across the y-axis
  • For -f(-x): Both reflections are applied
