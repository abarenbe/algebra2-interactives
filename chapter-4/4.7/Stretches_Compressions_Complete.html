Understanding Stretches & Compressions

Vertical (affects y-values)

y = a · f(x)
  • |a| > 1: Vertical stretch
  • 0 < |a| < 1: Vertical compression
  • a < 0: Also reflects over x-axis

Horizontal (affects x-values)

y = f(bx)
  • |b| > 1: Horizontal compression
  • 0 < |b| < 1: Horizontal stretch
  • b < 0: Also reflects over y-axis

Key Insight: Horizontal is "Backwards"

For horizontal transformations, the effect is opposite to what you might expect:

  • f(2x) makes the graph narrower (compressed by factor of 2)
  • f(½x) makes the graph wider (stretched by factor of 2)
