Understanding Turning Points

What is a Turning Point?

A turning point is where a polynomial function changes from increasing to decreasing (local maximum) or from decreasing to increasing (local minimum).

A polynomial of degree n has at most n - 1 turning points

Key Facts

  • Local maximum: highest point in a region (hill top)
  • Local minimum: lowest point in a region (valley bottom)
  • Degree 2: at most 1 turning point
  • Degree 3: at most 2 turning points
  • Degree 4: at most 3 turning points
