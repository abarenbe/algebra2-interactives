Section 4.8: Analyzing Graphs of Polynomial Functions

Master the art of reading and interpreting polynomial graphs. Learn to identify key features like turning points, zeros, end behavior, and intervals of increase and decrease.

Recommended Learning Path

  1. Start with End Behavior: Understanding how the degree and leading coefficient determine the graph's behavior at extreme values provides a foundation.
  2. Explore Zeros and Multiplicity: Learn how the multiplicity of zeros affects whether the graph crosses or touches the x-axis.
  3. Identify Turning Points: Discover local maxima and minima and understand the relationship between degree and turning points.
  4. Analyze Intervals: Put it all together by identifying where functions are increasing and decreasing.
