Understanding Descartes' Rule of Signs

What Is Descartes' Rule?

Descartes' Rule of Signs helps predict the number of positive and negative real roots of a polynomial without actually solving it.

Positive real roots: Count sign changes in f(x)

Negative real roots: Count sign changes in f(-x)

The Rule

  • The number of positive real roots equals the number of sign changes in f(x), or less by an even number
  • The number of negative real roots equals the number of sign changes in f(-x), or less by an even number
  • A sign change occurs when consecutive nonzero coefficients have opposite signs

Why Does This Work?

Each real root corresponds to a factor that crosses the x-axis. Complex roots come in pairs and don't affect the sign pattern. When we subtract by 2, we're accounting for pairs of complex conjugate roots that could replace real roots.

Quick Example

For f(x) = x³ - 2x² - 5x + 6:

+x³ → -2x² -5x → +6

Sign changes: 2 (+ to -, then - to +)

Positive real roots: 2 or 0
