Understanding Complex Conjugates

The Complex Conjugates Theorem

For a polynomial with real coefficients, if a + bi is a root (where b ≠ 0), then its complex conjugate a - bi must also be a root.

Remember: i = √(-1), so i² = -1

If a + bi is a root, then a - bi is also a root

Why Does This Work?

When a polynomial has real coefficients, the imaginary parts must cancel. The product of conjugates gives a real result:

(x - (a + bi))(x - (a - bi)) = x² - 2ax + (a² + b²)

The imaginary terms cancel because (bi)(-bi) = -b²i² = b²

Key Points

  • Only applies to polynomials with real coefficients
  • The conjugate changes the sign of the imaginary part only
  • Complex roots always come in pairs
  • A polynomial of odd degree must have at least one real root
