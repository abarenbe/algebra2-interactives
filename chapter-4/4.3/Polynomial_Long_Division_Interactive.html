Polynomial Long Division

Master the step-by-step process

How Long Division Works

Polynomial long division works just like regular long division with numbers. Follow these steps:

1

Divide

Divide leading terms

2

Multiply

Multiply divisor by result

3

Subtract

Subtract from dividend

4

Bring Down

Bring down next term

5

Repeat

Until degree < divisor

Key Points to Remember

  • Standard Form: Write both polynomials in descending order of powers
  • Placeholders: Use 0 for missing terms (e.g., x³ + 1 becomes x³ + 0x² + 0x + 1)
  • Leading Terms: Always divide the leading term of what remains by the leading term of the divisor
  • Signs: Be careful with negative signs when subtracting!

The Division Algorithm

When you divide polynomial f(x) by d(x), you get:

f(x) = d(x) · q(x) + r(x)

Where:

  • f(x) = Dividend (what you're dividing)
  • d(x) = Divisor (what you're dividing by)
  • q(x) = Quotient (the answer)
  • r(x) = Remainder (what's left over)
